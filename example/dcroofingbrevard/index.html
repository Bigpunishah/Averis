Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common roofing questions

How can I stay environmentally friendly with my roof?

There are many eco-friendly roof options including metal roof installation or clay tile roof installation. Both are made from recycled materials, are energy efficient, and have long lasting durability. Our experts can help you find the perfect eco-friendly style for your home and budget.

Which roof is best for my home and budget?

Asphalt shingle roofs are more affordable upfront but may require more frequent repairs. Metal roofs cost more initially but are more durable in severe weather, require less repairs, and last longer. Our expert roofing contractors can help guide you through all options to make the best decision for your home and budget.

How long does a roof last?

A roof should last a couple of decades depending on your climate and the materials used. The quality of installation also plays a major role in longevity. If you're unsure about your current roof's age, we offer professional consultations to determine if and when a roof replacement will be needed.

Do I need a permit to install a new roof?

Permit requirements vary by county and city. We help you navigate through all permit requirements and building code regulations. Even when permits aren't required, specific building codes must always be followed for safety and legal compliance.

What are signs that my roof has damage?

Look for water stains on ceilings or walls, broken or cracked shingles, and missing shingles. Check after severe weather or when moving into a home. If you have any doubts about roof damage, it's always best to have a licensed roofing contractor inspect your roof for safety.

My roof leaks, does it need to be replaced completely?

Not necessarily. There's no way to determine if a full roof replacement is needed without seeing the location and cause of the leak. Our professional roofing contractors can assess whether you need a small repair or a complete new roof installation.
